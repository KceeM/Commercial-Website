My Journey to Plant-Based Living

In 2020, during the global pandemic caused by COVID-19 - a highly contagious virus that led to respiratory illnesses, hospitalizations, and millions of deaths worldwide - I became increasingly concerned about my health.With people around me falling ill, I realized how vulnerable we all were. COVID-19 spreads primarily through respiratory droplets, and its severity varies, but those with underlying health conditions were particularly at risk.

In March 2020, I decided to take action by becoming pescatarian, which meant incorporating more plant-based meals into my diet and consuming chicken and fish instead of red meat. I had about 2-3 meatless meals a week, but cutting out red meat was one of the hardest adjustments I faced. After watching several documentaries and YouTube videos highlighting the health risks of red meat, including increased risks of heart disease, diabetes, high blood pressure, and certain cancers. I knew I had to make this change for my health.

As the pandemic worsened, so did my worries. In June 2020, my grandfather passed away, and soon after, I contracted COVID-19 at his funeral. The virus hit fast, and my family and I became very ill after that funeral. My mother, who already suffered from a chronic illness, was hospitalized due to severe complications. During this time, I lost my sense of taste, which ironically pushed me to fully embrace a vegan lifestyle. I realized that meat wasn’t helping me heal, and my body needed something different. I cooked a spicy vegan soup, which we all ate, and within 2/3 days, we began to gradually recover. My mom returned from the hospital after a week, she did not get to see us in the horrible state we were in. However, after recovering I knew from that moment on I would commit fully to this lifestyle.

Going vegan felt like a natural transition after starting as pescatarian. My body felt lighter, my energy improved, and even my mental clarity became sharper. However, living in South Africa, where most people don’t understand or support plant-based living, it hasn’t been easy. Many people judge my choices, but I’ve remained steadfast. It has been about 4 years in this journey and I actually transitioned to vegetarian two years ago, mainly because of cheese and I love baking and found it challenging to switch entirely to plant-based ingredients while still living at home. I kept vegan principles in many areas of my life such as drinking almond milk or soy milk and maintained a plant-focused diet overall.

This lifestyle has completely transformed me. It made me more mindful, energized, and healthy, even in a country that doesn’t always support this way of living. That’s why I created this website, to inspire others to explore plant-based living. Even small changes, like having meatless meals a few times a week or joining the lifestyle with a cheat meal once a month, it can significantly impact your health and the planet.

The truth is, I used to love the taste of meat, but after going vegan, I found it unpleasant, which taught me that the body can adapt and change. I encourage you to give it a try, whether it’s vegan, vegetarian, or just cutting down on meat. You’re making a step towards better health, and trust me...

Your body will thank you later!
